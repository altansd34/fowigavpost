What is Andrew Tate's kickboxing record? A closer look at the former professional kickboxer

August 2024 · 9 minute read

Andrew Tate is a British-American businessman, media personality, and former professional kickboxer. Some of his main businesses are Tate Media (a digital marketing service company) and Hustlers University (an educational platform). Andrew Tate's kickboxing record and investments earned him massive fame in the global business and sports domains.

Andrew Tate began practicing kickboxing in 2005, gained his first championship in 2009, and won four titles across two weight classes in ISKA kickboxing before quitting the sport in 2020. He became a global sensation in 2016 after appearing on the British reality show Big Brother on Channel 5. Besides being a reality show star and a former kickboxer, many know Andrew Tate as one of the most controversial social media personalities.

Who are Andrew Tate's parents?

Andrew Tate's English mother, Eileen, worked as a catering assistant, while his African-American father father, Emory Tate (1958–2015), was an international chess master. Kickboxing career

While working in the TV advertising industry, Tate began to practice kickboxing, boxing, and other martial arts in 2005. He debuted his kickboxing career with a defeat to Scott Gibson on 7 April 2007, and in November 2008, ISKA ranked him the seventh-best light heavyweight kickboxer in the UK.

The athlete won his first championship - the British ISKA Full Contact Cruiserweight Championship - in 2009. Eventually, Andrew was ranked No. 1 in his division in Europe. In 2011, he won his first ISKA world title in a rematch against Jean-Luc Benoit via knockout. On top of this, Tate was ranked the second-best light-heavyweight kickboxer in the world before losing the Enfusion championship tournament to Franci Grajš in 2012.
