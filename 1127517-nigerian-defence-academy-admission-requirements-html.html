Nigerian Defence Academy admission, requirements, application portal 2024/2025

August 2024 · 7 minute read

Admission into the Nigerian Defence Academy (NDA) is very competitive. The NDA admits both males and females into the Regular Combatant Course each year. There are certain requirements for you to be eligible for entry. Here is all you need to know about the Nigerian Defence Academy admission requirements.

Nigerian Defence Academy is a military institution where young, agile men and women are trained into well-educated, virile, courageous, and erudite subalterns. It runs a five-year Regular Combatant Course programme. At the end of the programme, you will earn a Bachelor's degree and a presidential commission to second lieutenant rank.

Nigerian Defence Academy

The Nigerian Defence Academy (NDA) was founded on the 5th of February, 1964, to train officers for the armed forces of Nigeria. The academy provides each officer cadet with the skills, knowledge, and values needed to meet a military officer's requirements in Nigeria.

What are the requirements for NDA admission?

For you to be able to gain admission into the Nigerian Defence Academy (NDA) Regular Combatant Course programme, you must meet the following NDA requirements:

  • You must physically and medically fit with proof and have good moral character.
  • You must be single without legal obligations to support another individual during your training.
  • You must have a certificate of state of origin from your local government office or a certificate of state indigene from your state governor's office.
  • The age for admission into the academy is between 17 and 21 years old at the time of admission.
  • You must have completed your Senior School Certificate Examination (WAEC or NECO) or a GCE O' Level with at least five credit passes in not more than two sittings. With a minimum of credit pass in Mathematics and English Language.
  • Must have passed the Unified Tertiary Matriculation examinations (UTME) and attained an acceptable minimum national standard (as determined by the JAMB annual policy meeting).
  • You must have a height of 1.68 meters and above if you are a male applicant or 1.58 meters if you are a female.

Important notification for applicants is that:

  • Your application will not be accepted or processed if you submit your form without your O'Level result.
  • Applicants with awaiting results cannot apply.
  • Only results that are a maximum of five years old from the date the examination was taken will be accepted.
  • Admission into the Academy is for both male and female Nigerians only.

How do you register for NDA online?

Once you meet the requirements, you can open the NDA application portal and apply. Here is a simple guide to the seamless application process for admission at NDA.

  • Go to the official application portal.
  • Create an account by providing your name, email address, and phone number and creating your unique password.
  • Follow the instructions sent to your email to activate the account. Once activated, log in using your email and the password you created.
  • Navigate to the application portal, choose "Purchase Access Code", and pay N3,500.00 via REMITA Retrieval Reference (RRR). Ensure payments align with RRR codes generated from the application portal. Alternatively, copy the RRR and make the payment through a bank.
  • Continue with the application process by entering the required details.
  • Confirm that all the information is accurate and submit your application.
  • After submission, download your acknowledgement form.
  • If your application is shortlisted, choose an examination venue as required during the application process.

    Screening test date and requirements

    Phase one of the post-UTME screening, known as the screening test, is set to take place on Saturday, the 25th of May, 2024. All eligible candidates must come prepared with the following:

    • Acknowledgement form.
    • Screening test admission card.
    • JAMB result slip.
    • Two postcard-size photographs (3.5 x 5 inches). The pictures should capture only the chest upwards and must include the candidate's name, exam number, state, exam centre, course of study, and signature at the back.

    Note: Only successful candidates in the NDA screening test will receive invitations for the armed forces selection board, marking phase 2 of the screening test. Following this, the list of admitted candidates will be made public.

    Can 21-year-olds apply for NDA?

    The age limit for applying to the Nigerian Defence Academy (NDA) is typically between 17 and 21 years at the time of application.

    How many years is NDA training in Nigeria?

    The Nigerian Defence Academy (NDA) training duration is typically five years (four years academic and one year military).

    Upon completing the training, graduates will be conferred with an honours degree in a core discipline (BSc, BEng, BA). Additionally, they will receive a Presidential Commission into the Nigerian Armed Forces.

    How can you pass the NDA screening?

    The NDA screening involves written examinations that test your knowledge in mathematics, English, and general knowledge. Review your high school-level mathematics and English, and stay updated on current affairs for the general knowledge section.

    How many uniforms does the NDA have?
